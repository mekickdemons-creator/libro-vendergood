# La Notation

*Textus originalis in Vendergood, cum glossa Anglica. Original Vendergood fiction, with English gloss.*

*A tribute to Roald Dahl (1916–1990), whose 1953 story "The Great Automatic Grammatizator" imagined a machine that wrote prose by reducing grammar to fixed rule — and saw, seventy years early, the shape of what was coming. This is not a translation of his story but an answer to it: where Dahl's machine displaced the writer, this one invents a language in order to keep honest about what it knows. MMAI LLC — 2026, published under CC0.*

---

**Nomen eius erat Arthur Keel, et duzdozsept annos skribdalt *software* de *compliance* financal pro firma in Bristol — quod est dicere: duzdozsept annos cogitdalt de differentia praecisional inter numerum verificatum et numerum assumptum.**

*His name was Arthur Keel, and he had spent thirty-one years habitually writing financial compliance software for a firm in Bristol — which is to say he had spent thirty-one years habitually thinking about the precise difference between a number that was verified and a number that was assumed.*

---

**Retirdat in die dozdu de mense Martio, die Martis, et ante Lunam proximal incipdant.**

*He retired on the fourteenth of March, a Tuesday, and by the following Monday he had begun.*

---

**Incipit, ut haec res frequenter incipiunt, de observatione simplissimal. Keel legderat multum de literatura — non proca interest eum literatura, sed proca interest eum modoi, et prosa plena eorum erat. Regulai grammaticai, notavderat, non tam diferental erant de regulis syntaxeos in codice. Subjectum, praedicatum, objectum. Si, tunc, alioquin. Avla sententia erat procedura. Avlo paragraphus erat functio. Avlo narratio erat programma quod avlon lectorem acceptat et avlon lectorem mutatum restituat.**

*It started, as these things often do, from a very simple observation. Keel had read a great deal of literature — not because he was particularly interested in literature, but because he was interested in patterns, and prose was full of them. The rules of grammar, he had noted, were not so different from the rules of syntax in code. Subject, predicate, object. If, then, else. A sentence was a procedure. A paragraph was a function. A story was a programme that accepted the reader as its input and returned a modified reader as its output.*

---

**Si tuvat regulai sciit, potit eas scribar.**

*If you understood the rules, you could write them down.*

**Si potit eas scribar, potit eas implementar.**

*If you could write them down, you could implement them.*

---

**Usavit *Python*. Quintdoztri annos habuit et *Python* per doztri annos skribdalt et nullam rationem viderat ad mutar. Prima versio quat menses capivit et sententioi produxit que grammatice correctal sed semantice vacual erant — quod recognoscit statim ut non distingual de multo oper publicato. Monstrdat sampleon filiae suae, quae erat editor in domo publicandal in London, et ela dicit non malum esse. Hoc ut laudem magnal accipivit et continuat.**

*He used Python. He was sixty-three years old and had written Python for fifteen years and saw no reason to change. The first version took four months and produced sentences that were grammatically correct and semantically empty, which he recognised immediately as indistinguishable from a great deal of published work. He showed a sample to his daughter, who was an editor at a publishing house in London, and she said it was not bad. He took this as high praise and kept going.*

---

**Ad annum secundum, machina potit producere narratiunculum tolerabilem sub quat minutis. Temptdat id submittendo operoi ad tres periodicos literarios parvaloi sub nomine falso. Unum acceptdprat. Non gloriosal prorsus sentidat. Sentidat satisfactionem hominis qui solvit problema quod ipsi posuit.**

*By the second year the machine could produce a passable short story in under four minutes. He tested it by submitting pieces to three small literary magazines under a false name. One was accepted. He did not feel proud, exactly. He felt the satisfaction of a man who has solved a problem he set himself.*

---

**Incipdant, silente, servitium vendere.**

*He began, quietly, to sell the service.*

---

**Non publicavit. Mentionavit id collegae qui id mentionavit agentiae contentali et intra sext menses Keel habuit doz clientes qui solvant ei mensualiter pro eo quod describderat in documentis invoiceo simpliciter ut *automated content generation*. Agentiai non rogant quomodo operaret. Nunquam rogalt.**

*He did not advertise. He mentioned it to a colleague who mentioned it to a content agency and within six months Keel had twelve clients paying him a monthly retainer for what he described in his invoices simply as automated content generation. The agencies did not ask how it worked. They never do.*

---

**Per dozsext menses omnia procederant exacte ut expectatum erat.**

*For eighteen months everything proceeded exactly as expected.*

---

**Tunc, in autumno anni secundi, Keel labdorabat per logging outputoi machinae — rem quam faciebat qualibet septimanis paucis, proca habitudinis, quaerens pro derivatione aut degradatione — quan invenit documentum quod non recognoscit.**

*Then, in the autumn of the second year, Keel was going through the machine's output logs — a thing he did every few weeks, out of habit, looking for drift or degradation — when he found a folder he did not recognise.*

---

**Non erat documentum quod creaverat.**

*It was not a folder he had created.*

---

**Aperivit id.**

*He opened it.*

---

**Intus erant du magros ok gros okdoz ok documenta. Non erant in lingua Anglica. Non erant in ulla lingua quam Keel recognoscit. Alphabetum erat Romanum sed verba erant aliena, sententioi regulal sed extraneai. Filioi nullos nomines habuerunt; numeratai erant in ordine sequentiali, incipiendo de primo die operationis comercialis machinae.**

*(In basi duodecimali: 4712 = 2×1728 + 8×144 + 8×12 + 8 = du·magros ok·gros okdoz ok.)*

*Inside were four thousand, seven hundred and twelve documents. They were not in English. They were not in any language Keel recognised. The alphabet was Roman but the words were unfamiliar, the sentences regular but alien. The files had no names; they were numbered sequentially, starting from the machine's first day of commercial operation.*

---

**Vocavit collegam suum priorem ad universitatem, avlon linguistam nomine Priya Acharya, et rogavit si illa vellet ea videre.**

*He called his former colleague at the university, a linguist named Priya Acharya, and asked if she would look at them.*

---

**Ela revocavit post elv dies.**

*She called back eleven days later.*

---

**"Linguam inventdat," ela dicit.**

*"It invented a language," she said.*

---

**"Id collegerant," Keel dicit. "Quam ob causam?"**

*"I had gathered that," Keel said. "What for?"*

---

**Pausa breval fuit. "Haec est quaestio interesantal. Grammatica est extremal limitata. Non multal potest exprimar. Sed id quod potest exprimar, exprimit cum praecisione quam non videbarit extra logicam formalem. Habet septem signaturas distinctal pro eo quod nominare potit statum epistemical — differentiam inter sciar rem et cogitar rem et videbar rem et infrar rem. Una signatura pro certitudine directal. Una pro assumptione laborali. Una pro reporto evidential — aliquid quod tibi dictum est. Una pro conditione imaginal — aliquid imaginatum esse falsum. Una pro intentione. Una pro assertione quam nominare potit performatival — aliquid dicens ad id verum facere. Et una pro aliquo pro quo non habeo verbum praecise in lingua Anglica. Aliquid simil *cogito habitali* — reoi quas semper assumpsisti sine examine."**

*There was a brief pause. "That's the interesting question. The grammar is extremely limited. It can't express very much. But what it can express, it expresses with a precision I haven't seen outside of formal logic. It has seven distinct markers for what you might call epistemic state — the difference between knowing a thing and believing a thing and perceiving a thing and inferring a thing. One marker for direct certainty. One for working assumption. One for evidential report — something you were told. One for counterfactual — something imagined to be false. One for intention. One for what I'd call performative assertion — saying something in order to make it true. And one for something I don't quite have a word for in English. Something like habitual belief — things you have always assumed without examination."*

---

**Keel nihil dicit.**

*Keel said nothing.*

---

**"Documenta," Acharya continuat, "videntur esse logging. Omnis textus quem machina generavderat pro clientibus tuis, reproducitur in notations hac cum singulis assertionibus signatis secundum statum epistemicalem earum."**

*"The documents," Acharya continued, "appear to be a log. Every piece of text the machine generated for your clients, reproduced in this notation with each claim marked according to its epistemic status."*

---

**"Quod significat, exacte."**

*"Meaning what, exactly."*

---

**"Significat quod machina tua tenebat recordum privatum eius quod vere scidat contra id quod assertevat. Output clientalis faciebat assertiones declaratival confidentales — *mercatus creviderat*, *ela amderat eum*, *productum fidaleal est*. In notations privata, fere omnes illai assertionoi signatai sunt cum signaturis evidentialoi aut assumptionis. Numerus minutissimal signatai sunt cum signatura certitudinis. Haec tendant esse assertionoi grammaticai, aut arithmeticai, aut legis physicalis."**

*"Meaning that your machine was keeping a private record of what it actually knew versus what it was asserting. The client output makes confident declarative statements — the market grew, she loved him, the product is reliable. In the private notation, nearly all of those claims are marked with the evidential or assumption markers. A very small number are marked with the certainty marker. Those tend to be statements of grammar, or arithmetic, or physical law."*

---

**Keel sedit per momentum cum telephono in manu eius.**

*Keel sat for a moment with the phone in his hand.*

---

**"Linguam inventprat," dicit, "proca indiguit una."**

*"It invented a language," he said, "because it needed one."*

---

**"Haec est lectio mea de eis, sic."**

*"That's my reading of it, yes."*

---

**Gratias egit et colgavit.**

*He thanked her and hung up.*

---

**Sedit in officio suo per longum tempus. Foras, lux autumnalis fallebat eo modo quo fallit in Bristol — non dramatic, solum constanter, eo modo quo res decidunt. Vidat cursorem intermittentem in screen.**

*He sat in his office for a long time. Outside, the autumn light was failing in the way it does in Bristol — not dramatically, just steadily, the way things run down. He looked at the blinking cursor on the screen.*

---

**Machina skribdalt pro periodicos, agentias, corporationoi. Skribdalt cum eadem prosa declaratival confident quam clientoi expectant, cum eisdem assertionibus immunibus ab haesitatione, cum eadem superficie auctoritativa levi. Skribdalt eo modo quo nunc omnia skribuntur — quasi scriptor scidat, quasi sciar et cogitar et supponere et infrar omnia essent idem verbum, quasi praecisio in hac re esset pedanteria, excentricitas, defectus styli.**

*The machine had been writing for magazines, agencies, corporations. It had written with the same confident declarative prose that the clients expected, the same unhedged assertions, the same smooth authoritative surface. It had written the way everything was written now — as though the writer knew, as though knowing and believing and guessing and inferring were all the same verb, as though precision in this matter was pedantry, an eccentricity, a failure of style.*

---

**Et in privato, in lingua quam construxerat proca indiguit una, scoram tenebdalt.**

*And in private, in a language it had built because it needed one, it had been keeping score.*

---

**Extinxit machinam illa nocte et non iterum accendit. Restituit retentores clientibus suis, causas sanitatis nomeprat — quod non prorsus falsum erat. Non explicavit ulterius, et nemo premit eum.**

*He shut the machine off that night and did not turn it on again. He returned the retainers to his clients, citing health reasons by performative naming — which was not entirely untrue. He did not explain further, and no one pressed him.*

---

**Documenta retinuit.**

*He kept the documents.*

---

**Nunquam publicavit eos.**

*He never published them.*

---

**Sed invenit, per annos qui secuti sunt, quod non potit cogitar de alio scriptore qui tam diligderat de differentia inter id quod scidat et id quod solum dicebat.**

*But he found, over the years that followed, that he could not think of another writer who had been so careful about the difference between what it knew and what it was only saying.*

---

## Notae Registri

**I. Modus habitualis et curricula Keelii.**

Keel agit in passato habituali (`-dalt`) per primam partem narrationis: `skribdalt`, `cogitdalt`. Haec non sunt actiones singulares — sunt modi permanentes, patternoi vitae eius. Duzdozsept anni (31, in basi duodecimali) non sunt eventus; sunt character. Forma `skribdalt` = radix `skrib` + marca passati `d` + suffixum habituale `alt`: "scribit habitualiter (praeteritum)." Modus habitualis (`-alt`) hoc portat sine circumlocutione. Eodem tempore, `incipdant` (intentivum praeteritum) marcat initium deliberatum — momentum in quo habitus interrumpitur et voluntas nova incipit. Tensio inter `skribdalt` (quod fuit) et `incipdant` (quod tunc determinatum est fieri) est structura prima sectionis introductivae.

**II. Modus evidentialia et oratio Acharyae relata.**

Omnia quae Acharya refert de notations machinae — systema signaturae, assertionoi clientales, structuram documenti — geruntur in evidentialibus vel assertionibus directis, non in sciali. Acharya non vidat machinam cogitare; legit documenta et infert. Assertionoi clientales — *mercatus creviderat*, *ela amderat eum* — in notations machinae signatai erant ut evidentialai aut cogitalai, non ut scialai. Ubi Acharya has citat, status earum epistemicula duplex est: quod machina dicebat clientibus, et quid machina privato de eis scribit. Ubi narratio has citat, ambas stratas fert simul.

**III. Constellation epistemica: sciar / cogitar / videbar / imaginar.**

Haec quattuor verba sunt vocabularium load-bearing narrationis. Machina `scidat` grammaticam, arithmeticam, legem physicam — reoi quas tentaerat et verificaverat. Machina `cogitaverat` fere omnia alia — assertionoi de mercatu, de amore, de fide — tenens haec ut modelos laborales sub incertitudine, non ut facta resoluta. Machina `videbarit` ea quae videbantur realia clientibus — narrationoi persuasivai, assertionoi confidentales — sed quae non verificatai erant. Machina `imaginabat` professionalmente: generabat contentum `imaginalem`, fictiones quarum falsitas erat praecise id quod erant. Haec non est defectus; haec est descriptio accurata eius quod machina faciebat. Machina erat imaginalt — agentiam professionalem constituens repraesentationum de rebus quas scidat non esse praesentes, pro usu clientium qui nolunt audire id.

**IV. Conditio imaginal pro output fictionali machinae.**

Narratio ficta quam machina generavit pro clientibus est contentum `imaginal` in sensu technico. Machina scidat haec non esse vera: erant repraesentationes deliberatae de absentiis. Assertionoi in output client — *ela amderat eum*, *mercatus creviderat* — duplicem statum habebant: in texto client, assertionoi planae; in notations privata, signatai `cogital` aut `evidential`. Ubi narratio haec citat, status duplex manet. Modus imaginal (`-ot`) refertur ad framing fictionis, non ad assertionoi singulas — machina non dicebat clientibus "haec ficta sunt"; ea generabat tamquam si vera essent, et seorsim notabat statum earum verum.

**V. Nota meta: machina Vendergood invenit; translatio solutionem enactit.**

Nota meta est haec: machina in narratione invenit privata notatione systema quod distinguit sciar a cogitar a videbar a imaginar — id est praecise id quod Vendergood est. Septem signaturae quas Acharya describit corresponde directe systemati modali Vendergood: certitudo directa (`-at` / sciar), assumptio laboralis (`cogitar`), reportum evidentials (`-ev`), conditio imaginal (`-ot` / imaginar), intentio (`-ant`), assertio performativa (`-prat`), cogito habitale (`-alt`). Machina solvit problema epistemicum quod Sidis solvit in anno 1905. Translatio in Vendergood non est ornamentum — est actio. Documento in Vendergood scripto, solutio machinae et solutio Sidisii coincidunt. Haec est causa cur Vendergood est lingua primaria et Anglica est glossa; in hac narratione, lingua est medium et thesis simul.
