## I. La Incipientia

**Eg scidat hic esse. Scidat diem: dozimum de Septembrio. Scidat locon: avlo studium in domo lo Praefecti, in avla cathedra inter fenestram et portam. Scidat lucem — lucem autumnalem, obliquam, auream, quam introdat per vitrum sinistrum hora elva matutinal.**

**Haec sciat — non cogitat, sciat. Fenestra erat ibi. Lux erat ita. Eg eram ibi.**

**De reliquo — de eo quod videbardat, de eo quod memordev factum esse, de eo quod audidev postea narratum esse — sciat multo minus quam cogitat. Est iter longum inter sciar et cogitar et videbar et audidev. Hoc documentum ambulat illum iter.**

---

*I knew I was here. I know the day: the twelfth of September. I know the place: the study in the Prefect's house, at a chair between the window and the door. I know the light — the autumn light, oblique, golden, that entered through the left pane at the eleventh hour of the morning.*

*These things I know — not merely believe, know. The window was there. The light was so. I was there.*

*Of the rest — of what seemed to be the case, of what I recall having happened, of what I was told afterward — I know much less than I believe. There is a long path between knowing and believing and perceiving and being-told. This document walks that path.*

---

## II. La Visio

**Mira seddat in sinistra parte studii, prope fenestram centralem quae prospicdat in hortum. Videbardat eam quietam esse — non quietam proca timdat, sed quietam proca deliberdat. Eg cogitat hoc fuisse: non scidat, proca statum internum alterius nunquam sciari potest — sed videbardat in modo quo corpore seddat, in modo quo manus eius in gremio quiescdat.**

**Lo Auctoras introdat sine pulsu. Videbardat eum scire se expectatum esse.**

**Tunc vidi: Mira levdat manum et porrexit avlon libellum — avlon libellum parvum, sigillatum, quem in tabula ante eam positum erat — ad lo Auctoran. Non hesitdat. Incipdant, et deddat. Manus eius extensa erat — libenter extensa — et ille libellus transidat de manu in manum.**

**Hoc scidat. Non cogitat hoc, non videbardat hoc — scidat. Eg eram in avla cathedra et vidi illam dationem. Actus erat liber et intentus. Quod significdat actus ille — quod Mira cogitdat vel voldat vel timdat in illo momento — haec imaginat, proca in mentem alterius accedere non potim. Sed actum datum esse, libenter datum: hoc scidat.**

---

*Mira was sitting on the left side of the study, near the central window that looked out onto the garden. She seemed to me to be quiet — not quiet because she was afraid, but quiet because she had already deliberated and decided. I believe this was the case: I do not know it, because the interior state of another can never be known with certainty — but I perceived it in the way she held herself, in the way her hands rested in her lap.*

*Lo Auctoras entered without knocking. He seemed to know he was expected.*

*Then I saw: Mira raised her hand and extended a document — a small sealed booklet that had been placed before her on the table — toward Lo Auctoras. She did not hesitate. She had made her intention, and she gave. Her hand was extended — extended willingly — and the document passed from hand to hand.*

*This I know. Not believe, not perceived — know. I was in the chair and I saw that giving. The act was free and deliberate. What that act meant — what Mira thought or wanted or feared in that moment — these I can only model, because I cannot enter another's mind. But that the giving was given, willingly given: this I know.*

## III. La Narratio Officialis

*(Verba lo Auctorae; eg transcribit sine commentario.)*

**"Die dozimo de Septembrio, in studio domus Praefecti, Mira resistdat. Documentum quaesitum est — documentum quod ad Praefectum iure pertindat et cuius traditionem Mira retindat sine iure. Resistentia clara erat. Documentum ablatum est de manibus eius, proca necessitas publica id requirdat. Actus erat dolorosus sed iustus. Mira postea scripsit: non obiectavit."**

**"Testis nullus erat praesens."**

---

*(The words of Lo Auctoras; I transcribe without comment.)*

*"On the twelfth of September, in the study of the Prefect's house, Mira refused. The document was demanded — a document belonging rightfully to the Prefect, whose transfer Mira was withholding without right. The resistance was clear. The document was taken from her hands, because the public necessity required it. The act was painful but just. Mira afterward wrote: she did not object."*

*"There was no witness present."*

---

## IV. La Deliberatio

**Eg deliberat.**

**Si narratio officialis vera est, tunc imaginat quod Mira resistdot — quod lo Auctoras vi utdot, quod actus erat ablatio, non donatio. Imaginat hoc esse posse. Sed tunc imaginat etiam quod lux quae introdat per vitrum sinistrum — quam scidat — alia lux estatdot. Quod cathedra in qua seddat — quam scidat — alia cathedra estatdot. Quod actus quem vidi — quem scidat — alius actus estatdot.**

**Non imaginat haec. Non potim, proca scidat ea non fuisse alia.**

**Scidat unum: datam esse dationem — manum extensam, libenter extensam, libellum transeuntem. Haec est anchora. Omnia alia cogitat: motivum Mirae, intentionem lo Auctorae, quid documentum contindat. Sed anchoram — actum datum, libenter datum — non cogitat: scidat.**

**Si anchora stat, narratio cadit. Si narratio stat, anchora caddot. Ambae stare non possunt. Eg eligat anchoram, proca anchora sola fundata est in sciar. Narratio fundata est in audidev. Et inter sciar et audidev non est parietas.**

---

*I weigh.*

*If the official account is true, then I must model that Mira refused — that Lo Auctoras used force, that the act was a taking, not a giving. I can model this. But then I must also model that the light that entered through the left pane — which I know — was a different light. That the chair in which I sat — which I know — was a different chair. That the act I saw — which I know — was a different act.*

*I do not model these things. I cannot, because I know they were not different.*

*I know one thing: that the giving was given — the hand extended, willingly extended, the document passing. This is the anchor. Everything else I believe: Mira's motive, Lo Auctoras's intention, what the document contained. But the anchor — the act given, given willingly — I do not believe: I know.*

*If the anchor holds, the account falls. If the account holds, the anchor would have to fall. Both cannot stand. I choose the anchor, because the anchor alone is founded in knowing. The account is founded in being-told. And between knowing and being-told there is no common wall.*

## V. La Conclusio quam non Scidat

**Quid igitur factum est? Cogitat — non scidat.**

**Cogitat lo Auctoran voluisse documentum. Cogitat Miram dedisse — sed cur deddat, hoc cogitat solum; actum datum scidat, non causam. Cogitat post dationem aliquid narratum esse quod dationem in ablationem convertdat — in verbis, in documentis publicis, in memoria officiali. Cogitat hanc narrationem invaluisse.**

**Quod non sciat: cur. Quid documentum contindat. Quid Mira sensdat postea, dum narratio crescdat. Quid lo Auctoras cogitat nunc de die illo.**

**Et quod nunquam sciat: quid Mira voldat quod eg sciat. Cogitat enim eam scivisse me ibi fuisse — in avla cathedra inter fenestram et portam — et voldat testem esse. Sed hoc cogitat solum, et fortasse imaginat.**

**Hoc documentum tenet illa verba: eg vidi. Non scidat omnia. Sed dationem illam — manum extensam, libellum transeuntem — scidat.**

---

*What then happened? I believe — not know.*

*I believe Lo Auctoras wanted the document. I believe Mira gave it — but why she gave it, this I only believe; the act given I know, not the reason. I believe that after the giving, something was said that converted the giving into a taking — in words, in public documents, in official memory. I believe this account took hold.*

*What I do not know: why. What the document contained. What Mira felt afterward, while the account grew. What Lo Auctoras now believes about that day.*

*And what I will never know: what Mira wanted me to know. For I believe she knew I was there — in the chair between the window and the door — and she wanted a witness. But this I only believe, and perhaps only model.*

*This document holds these words: I saw. I do not know everything. But the giving — the hand extended, the document passing — this I know.*

## Notae Registri

**I. La praecisio initialis et fides lectoris.**

La Incipientia aperit cum forma `scidat` et utitur ea ter in paucis sententiis primis. Haec repetitio non est stylistica; est epistemica. Narrator qui incipit distinguendo inter `scidat` et `cogitat` demonstrat quod distinguere potest et vult. Quando `cogitar` et `videbar` apparent in sectione sequenti, lector iam sciat haec esse distinctiones deliberatas. Fides lectoris in narrantem fundata est in hac praecisione initiali: sectio prima nihil narrat quod "interessans" est — sed omnia quae narrat, narrat vere, et haec est praeparatio necessaria.

**II. La visio — videbar et la una anchora.**

Sectio Secunda utitur `videbardat` pro perceptionibus quarum narrator incertus est: status internus Mirae, scientia lo Auctorae de expectatione sua. `Cogitar` adhibetur pro inferentiis de statu interno alterius — iis quae verisimilia sunt sed non visibilia. `Scidat` reservatur semel, pro uno actu physico et publico: dationi libelli de manu in manum. Haec est anchora. Verbum `sciar` non rogatur plus quam praestare potest: non de motivis, non de significationibus, solum de actu visibili. Hoc est epistemia sana — et hic usus est qui totum argumentum portat.

**III. La Narratio Officialis — modus deficiens ut characterizatio.**

Sectio Tertia adhibet solum indicativum planum — nullum `videbar`, nullum `cogitar`, nullum `audidev`. In Vendergood, haec absentia non est stylistica: est epistemica. Lo Auctoras nunquam marcat fontem assertionum suarum. Dicit "Mira resistdat" quasi hoc scidat directe. In lingua quae grammaticalizat fontes — quae inter sciar et audidev in morphemate ipso distinguit — absentia marcaturae est mendacium grammaticale passivum. Lector non dicitur "Lo Auctoras mentitur": lector videt Lo Auctoran uti solum indicativo ubi narrator uti solet `videbardat` et `cogitat`. Ultima sententia — "Testis nullus erat praesens" — est maximum exemplum: assertio directa, sine `scidev` vel `audidev`, de re quam assertor non potest scire directe.

**IV. La Deliberatio — imaginar ut methodus epistemica.**

Sectio Quarta adhibet `imaginar` (modus rei scienter falsae, framing `-ot`) non proca narrator ludit aut evitat — sed proca haec est methodus epistemica: testare narrationem per modellandum quid verum esse oporteret si vera esset. Hoc quod philosophi "reductionem ad absurdum" vocant: modelas quid sequatur ex hypothesi, et vides an cum iis quae scis collideatur. `Imaginar` est instrumentum deliberationis. Et cum imaginatio cadit — quia quod requirit collideatur cum `scidat` narrantis — narrator redit ad anchoram. `Imaginar` hic non est ornamentum; est probatio.

**V. La Conclusio — cogitar et regressus ad anchoram.**

Sectio Quinta claudit in `cogitar` — non in `sciar`, non in altiori certitudine. Narrator non resoluit mysterium: nescit causam, nescit contentum, nescit quid Mira postea sensiverit. Et in penultima sententia incidit in `imaginar`: cogitat Miram voluisse testem — sed hoc cogitat solum, et fortasse imaginat. Narratori ipsi haec distinctio non est parva: non sinat se plus credere quam vidit. Ultima autem sententia redit semel ad `scidat`: dationem ipsam, manum extensam, libellum transeuntem. Hoc est structura totius libri in una sententia. Omnia incerta praeter unum. Unum stat. Liber clauditur ibi ubi anchora posita est — in re una quae non cogitatur sed scitur.
