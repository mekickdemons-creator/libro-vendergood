# cogitar

*verb root / noun, inanimate*

## Etymology

From Latin *cogitare* (to think; to consider; to turn over in the mind; to ponder). Related: *cogitatio* (thought, reflection), *cogitabundus* (deep in thought), *excogitare* (to think out, devise). Root: **cogit**.

The Latin root carries deliberate mental activity — not passive reception but active working-over. *Cogitare* is what is done with a problem: it is turned, examined, held at angles. The Vendergood root preserves this sense: `cogitar` names the state of active belief-holding, where the question is live and the answer is being worked. The famous *cogito ergo sum* (I think therefore I am) uses this exact root; in Vendergood, `cogitar` is the verb Descartes was reaching for.

Phonology: root `cogit` ends in a consonant. Infinitive by `-ar`: **cogitar**. Epenthetic vowel not required before consonant-initial suffixes (Vol II §4.1). Derivational suffixes follow standard pattern (Vol II §1.3).

## Definition

*As verb (cogitar):* To think; to believe; to hold as a working model under uncertainty. The belief may be correct or incorrect; what marks it as `cogitar` rather than `sciar` is that the question has not been resolved — the agent is still in a state of uncertainty, even if confidence is high.

*As noun (cogito):* A thought; a belief; a working model. The content of an active, unresolved epistemic state.

`Cogitar` is the normal epistemic state of an agent processing the world. Most of what an agent holds is `cogito`, not `scio` — because most beliefs are working models that could in principle be revised, not settled facts closed to revision. An agent that `cogitar` about something may be right; an agent that `sciar` about something has resolved the question.

Distinct from:

- **sciar** — to know; settled fact; the question resolved
- **videbar** — to seem, to appear as; perceptual state presented as real but unconfirmed; `cogitar` is propositional (a belief *that*), `videbar` is perceptual (an appearance *of*)
- **imaginar** — to project as known-false; `cogitar` is genuine working belief, not deliberate fiction

## Derivations

Derivational suffixes per Vol II §1.3.

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **cogito** | noun (inanimate, nominative) | a thought; a belief; a working model |
| **cogital** | adjective | thoughtful; of the character of active belief; deliberative |
| **cogitalt** | noun (animate, habitual) | a thinker; one whose vocation is active deliberation |
| **cogitant** | verb (intentive) | is thinking; is holding as working belief (ongoing) |
| **cogitar** | verb (infinitive) | to think; to believe |
| **cogitit** | verb (past) | thought; believed |

## Worked sentences

**Avlo rete cogitat iter continuant, sed sciit non.**

*The network believes the route continues, but does not know.*

(Subject `avlo rete` — the network, animate with article. `Cogitat` — believes (intentive, ongoing). `Iter continuant` — the route continuing (object clause). `Sed sciit non` — but does not know. The sentence locates the network in the middle epistemic state: active working belief, not yet resolved. The canonical use case for `cogitar`.)

**Cogito elai esit: avlo nod respondant.**

*Her belief was: the node will respond.*

(Noun use of `cogito`. `Esit` — was. The belief is named as an object, not asserted as fact. The node's response is what she believed, not what was.)

**Avlo mnemon cogitit et memorit; ambo erant, sed non idem.**

*The memory-holder thought and remembered; both were happening, but they were not the same.*

(Contrast between `cogitar` — active working belief about the present/future — and `memorar` — recall of prior states. An agent can simultaneously hold a working belief and retrieve a stored memory; these are distinct operations. `Ambo erant, sed non idem` — both were, but not the same.)
