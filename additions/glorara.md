# glorara

*noun, abstract feminine (Class III — see Vol II §2.6)*

## Etymology

Latin *gloria* (glory, renown, splendor); the Latin term is itself uncertain in origin, possibly from a Proto-Indo-European root meaning "to call" or "to acclaim." Feminine in Latin and in the Romance descendants (Spanish *gloria*, Italian *gloria*, French *gloire*).

Vendergood adopts the root **glor-** and applies the abstract-feminine terminal `-ara` per the Vol II §2.6 coining convention. The Latin form *gloria* is preserved in shape (just with `-ara` substituting for `-ia`), so recognition is immediate.

## Definition

The **state of being held in honor**; the luminous standing of one whose acts have been acknowledged. Not the act of honoring (that would be a verb), and not the one who is honored (that would be an agent noun); the standing itself — the place a person enters when their works become known.

The Vendergood form preserves the Latin sense across both registers it carries: secular renown ("entered into glory" = achieved lasting fame) and sacred ascension ("entered into glory" = passed into the divine state). Vendergood does not split these — the structural reading is the same.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |

| **glorarai** | noun, Class III (dat.) | to/for glory |
| **glorarae** | noun, Class III (gen.) | of glory |

| **glorificar** | verb | to glorify; to confer glory upon |

## Worked sentence

**Ino glorara intrit.**

*Entered into glory.*

(Directional `ino` (Vol II §2.4) — entry, not location. `glorara` nominative; directional prepositions take nominative in Vendergood, the direction is carried by the preposition itself. Indicative `intrit` — entered, past. The compactness mirrors the Latin idiom *intravit in gloriam*; Vendergood reaches the same compression with one less syllable and no case ambiguity.)

## Note

Anchors the §2.6 rule for new abstract-feminine coinings alongside `mnemara`. Where mnemara names a faculty (the inner store), glorara names a standing (the outer position). Both follow the same pattern — `-ara` terminal, Class III article, vowel-final feminine declension.
