# animara

*noun, abstract feminine (Class III — see Vol II §2.6)*

## Etymology

Latin *anima* (soul, life-breath, animating principle); from Proto-Indo-European *\*h₂enh₁-* (to breathe), the same root as Greek *ἄνεμος* (anemos, wind) and Sanskrit *aniti* (breathes). Feminine in Latin; consistently feminine across the Romance descendants (Spanish *alma*, Italian *anima*, French *âme*).

Vendergood adopts the root **anim-** and applies the abstract-feminine terminal `-ara` per the Vol II §2.6 coining convention. The Latin form is preserved in shape; the `-ara` replaces the bare `-a` to mark the noun as Class III feminine on the Vendergood productive pattern.

## Definition

The **animating principle**; what makes a body alive rather than merely arranged. Not the body, not the mind, not the breath — the *something* whose presence is the difference between a living thing and the same matter dead.

Distinct from:

- **mnemara** — the faculty of memory (one of the animara's contents)
- **cogito** — a thought (a transient act of the animara)
- **vit-** (as in *vivit*, "lives") — the verb of being alive; animara is the noun-form of what is alive

The animara is what departs at death. The classical sense is preserved: at death, animara *ad caelo it* (goes to heaven) or returns to whatever cosmology the speaker holds. Vendergood does not commit to a metaphysics; the word names the structural role.

## Worked sentence

**Quan stello cadit, animara ad caelo it.**

*When a star falls, a soul goes to heaven.*

(Temporal `quan` (Vol II §2.5) — sequential when. `stello cadit` — a star falls (indicative). `animara ad caelo it` — soul goes to heaven; directional `ad` for destination; `caelo` nominative-after-preposition. The folk-belief inversion: the star's fall *causes* the soul's rising, or more precisely, the two events are taken to be paired. Vendergood preserves the paired-event reading without committing to causation.)

## Note

Like `glorara` and `mnemara`, anchors the §2.6 rule for new abstract-feminine coinings. The three together establish `-ara` as the productive Class III terminal across faculty (`mnemara`), standing (`glorara`), and principle (`animara`).
