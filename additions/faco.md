# faco

*noun, inanimate / verb root*

## Etymology

From Latin *fax* (genitive *facis*) — torch; firebrand; a piece of wood or material carrying fire; the source from which fire is taken and carried. Related: *facula* (small torch, small flame), *facula* (a bright point of light), *face* (archaic English: torch, from the same Latin root). Root: **fac**.

*Fax* in Latin named the carried light — not the fire burning in a fixed place (*ignis*, *flamma*) but the portable object that brings fire to a new place, that crosses a dark space and arrives with light. A torch carried in a procession, a brand used to light a funeral pyre, the flame passed from one lamp to another. The Vendergood root inherits this sense: `faco` is the thing that carries the potential for light, that when struck produces a moment of illumination.

In the Vendergood corpus, `faco` is the epistemic trigger. Every vision in the Little Match Girl is initiated by the striking of a match: *Ela ferit faco contra muro; quan flamit!* — she struck the match against the wall; how it blazed! Each `faco` opens a window of `videbar` — uncertain perception presented as real. When the match goes out (`faco extinxit`), the vision ends and the plain world returns. `Faco` is the object that makes perception uncertain.

Phonology: root `fac` ends in a consonant. Inanimate nominative by `-o`: **faco**. Infinitive by `-ar`: **facar**. Derivational suffixes follow standard pattern (Vol II §1.3).

## Definition

*As noun (faco):* A match; a firebrand; any small portable object that carries potential fire and, when activated, produces a brief, bright flame. In the corpus: specifically the sulfur match of the nineteenth century — small, struck against a rough surface, burning briefly.

*As verb (facar):* To kindle; to light from a carried flame; to bring fire to a new place by means of a portable source.

The `faco` is distinct from the fire it produces:

- **faco** — the match; the carrier of potential light
- **flamo** — the flame produced when the match is struck
- **igno** — fire in general; burning; the larger phenomenon
- **luco** — light; what the flame produces; the illumination

A `faco` produces `flamo`, which produces `luco`, which enables `videbar`. The chain is: the match is struck → a flame appears → the light makes the world transparent → the uncertain perception begins. Each link in this chain has its own word in Vendergood.

## Derivations

Derivational suffixes per Vol II §1.3.

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **faco** | noun (inanimate, nominative) | a match; a firebrand; a portable source of fire |
| **facal** | adjective | incendiary; of the character of fire-carrying; that which carries and transmits light |
| **facalt** | noun (animate, habitual) | one who carries fire; a light-bearer; one whose function is to bring illumination |
| **facant** | verb (intentive) | is kindling; is transmitting fire (ongoing) |
| **facar** | verb (infinitive) | to kindle; to light from a carried source |
| **facit** | verb (past) | kindled; lit |

*Note on `facalt`:* The animate habitual form — one who carries fire — resonates with the Latin *Lucifer* (light-bearer), though the etymology is distinct. The `facalt` carries light into dark places; what is found there is uncertain.

## Worked sentences

**Ela traxit faco pro fasco et ferit id contra muro.**

*She drew a match from the bundle and struck it against the wall.*

(From the Little Match Girl corpus. `Traxit` — drew out (past of *trahere*). `Pro fasco` — from the bundle. `Ferit` — struck (past). The action sequence: draw, strike. What follows is `videbar`.)

**Faco extinxit — et videbo evanit.**

*The match went out — and the appearance vanished.*

(The paired motion: match out, vision gone. `Extinxit` — went out (past). `Videbo` — the appearance (noun form of `videbar`). `Evanit` — vanished (past). In two clauses: the causal structure of every match scene in the corpus. The `faco` does not merely produce light — it produces the epistemic window. When it closes, `videbar` ends.)

**Avlo facalt portat luco in scuro — sed quan faco extinxit, sciit non quan vidit.**

*The light-bearer carries light into the dark — but when the match goes out, it is not known what was seen.*

(Animate subject `avlo facalt`. `Portat` — carries (intentive). `In scuro` — into the dark. `Sed quan faco extinxit` — but when the match goes out. `Sciit non quan vidit` — it is not known what was seen. The sentence names the epistemology of `faco`: the light was real, the seeing was real, but whether what was seen was real remains `videbar`, not `sciar`.)
