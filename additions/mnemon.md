# mnemon

*noun, animate*

## Etymology

Greek *μνήμων* (mnēmōn) — mindful, one who remembers; from *μνήμη* (mnēmē), memory. The Greek agent form is adopted directly: the word is its own derivation. Vendergood root: **mnem**.

Sidis documented Greek as a source language for Vendergood. The direct adoption of a Greek agent noun is consistent with the documented classical compositionality: where the source language already provides the right form, Vendergood inherits it.

## Definition

One who carries memory; the faculty of holding what has been perceived and making it retrievable. Specifically: the capacity that bridges past experience to present cognition.

Distinct from:

- `ken` — what is *currently* known; the content of knowledge as present state
- `mnemion` — a specific act of remembering
- the **fidifer** — faithful transmission to another; the mnemon holds *inward*, the fidifer carries *outward*

The mnemon is not a record. It is the living faculty that reaches back.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **mnemon** | noun, animate | memory-carrier; one who remembers (agent form, adopted from Greek) |
| **mnemion** | noun of act | the act of remembering; an act of memory |
| **mnemal** | adjective | of or relating to memory; mnemonic |
| **mnemalt** | noun, habitual | one whose vocation is faithful preservation of what has been perceived |

## Worked sentence

**Avlo mnemalt vidat et havet avlon mnemion — non nescit.**

*The keeper of memory sees and had the act of memory — does not not-know.*

(Indicative *vidat*: observes. Past *havet* — *hav-d-at*: held. Evidential *nescit*: does not know by report. The double negative *non nescit* is intentional: the mnemalt is precisely the one of whom it cannot be reported that they have forgotten.)
