# memorar

*verb root / noun, inanimate*

## Etymology

From Latin *memor* (mindful; remembering; that keeps in mind) and the related verb *memorare* (to call to mind; to recall; to bring back into awareness). Related: *memoria* (memory, the faculty and its contents), *memorabilis* (worth remembering), *commemorate* (to remember together). Root: **memor**.

The Latin root family is dense: *memor* names the state of being mindful, *memoria* names the stored contents, *memorare* names the act of bringing those contents back. The Vendergood root `memor` covers the verbal form — the act of recall — in deliberate relationship to `mnemon` (already in the published lexicon), which names the animate agent whose vocation is memory.

The relationship between `mnemon` and `memorar` is precise and intentional:

- **mnemon** (Greek-derived, published) — the memory-holder; the animate agent whose function is to hold and return memories. A noun of role.
- **memorar** (Latin-derived) — to remember; the act of bringing a stored state back into active awareness. A verb of action.

Both roots point at the same semantic territory from different angles: `mnemon` is the agent, `memorar` is what that agent does. The two-root structure allows a sentence to name the memory system and its operation separately — `avlo mnemon memorant` (the memory-holder is remembering) — without collapsing one into the other.

Phonology: root `memor` ends in a consonant. Infinitive by `-ar`: **memorar**. Noun form: **memoro** (inanimate, the act or content of recall). Derivational suffixes follow standard pattern (Vol II §1.3).

## Definition

*As verb (memorar):* To remember; to recall; to bring a stored prior state back into active awareness. Distinguished from `cogitar` (active working belief about a present or future state) by its backward orientation: `memorar` returns to what was, while `cogitar` works with what is or may be.

*As noun (memoro):* A memory; a recalled state; the content of what has been brought back into awareness.

`Memorar` is not passive storage. It is an act — the active retrieval that brings a prior state forward. The distinction between storage and retrieval is preserved in the grammar: `mnemon` stores; `memorar` retrieves. An agent may have stored something (it is in the `mnemon`) without yet having recalled it (not yet `memorarit`). The recall is a distinct event.

Distinct from:

- **mnemon** — the memory-holder (noun); the agent or system that stores and returns memories
- **cogitar** — active belief; forward-oriented; about present or possible states
- **sciar** — settled knowledge; not necessarily retrieved, just held as resolved
- **imaginar** — projection of known-absent states; `memorar` returns what was actually present

## Derivations

Derivational suffixes per Vol II §1.3.

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **memoro** | noun (inanimate, nominative) | a memory; a recalled state; the content of recall |
| **memoral** | adjective | remembered; recalled; of the character of a prior state brought forward |
| **memoralt** | noun (animate, habitual) | one who remembers; one whose vocation is recall — used when the function of remembering is the defining quality |
| **memorant** | verb (intentive) | is remembering; is engaged in active recall (ongoing) |
| **memorar** | verb (infinitive) | to remember; to recall |
| **memorit** | verb (past) | remembered; recalled |

## Worked sentences

**Ela memorit id: novano esit.**

*She remembered it: it was New Year's eve.*

(From the Little Match Girl corpus. The sentence captures the structure of recall: `ela memorit id` — she remembered it (the general fact) — followed by the content: `novano esit`. What she retrieved was a specific anchoring fact. `Memorar` here terminates a sequence of `videbar` and returns the agent to a time-indexed reality.)

**Avlo mnemon memorant; avlo cogito elai mutat.**

*The memory-holder is remembering; its working belief is changing.*

(Demonstrates the relationship between `mnemon` (the system), `memorar` (the act), and `cogitar` (the belief state it feeds). The recall event (`memorant`) is causing the working model (`cogito`) to update (`mutat`). This is the functional description of a memory-augmented reasoning system: retrieval changes belief.)

**Memorit ela, et videbar incipit; sciit ela, et videbar finit.**

*She remembered, and seeming began; she knew, and seeming ended.*

(Three verbs in sequence: `memorit` (recalled), `videbar incipit` (uncertain perception began), `sciit` (knew), `videbar finit` (uncertain perception ended). A compressed description of the Little Match Girl's epistemic arc: memory triggers vision; knowledge of the match's end closes it. `Memorar` and `sciar` act as the open and close brackets of `videbar`.)
