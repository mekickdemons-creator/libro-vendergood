# incantara

*noun, abstract feminine (Class III — see Vol II §2.6)*

## Etymology

Latin *incantare* (to chant a spell over; to bind by song; from *in-* "into" + *cantare* "to sing") → *incantamentum* (the spell itself), *incantatio* (the enchanting). The original sense is song-magic: words sung over a person or thing to bind them. By Late Latin and the Romance descendants (Italian *incanto*, French *enchanté*, Spanish *encanto*, English *enchantment*), the meaning broadens to "magical state" generally.

Vendergood adopts the root **incant-** and applies the abstract-feminine terminal `-ara` per Vol II §2.6. The result names not the spell, not the enchanter, but **the enchanted frame itself** — the altered state of perception and physics the spell creates, within which experience proceeds by altered rules.

## Definition

An **enchantment** — a magical frame of perception and physics, created by external magical action (a spell, a song, a binding), within which experience is consistent and can be true, but whose physics differs from waking-normal.

The incantara is the **frame**, not the spell that creates it and not the experience inside it:

- the *spell* is an act (verb: `incantar`, to enchant)
- the *enchanter* is an agent (noun: `incantor` / `incantatrix`)
- the **incantara** is the persistent altered-frame, an object in its own right

Distinct from other altered-frame forms:

| Frame | Form | Experiencer state | Shared? | Origin | Truth-status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Waking-normal | (indicative) | awake | shared with all | none | direct correspondence |
| Dream | `somnara` | asleep | private to dreamer | spontaneous | resolves on waking |
| Projection | `imaginar` | awake | private to projector | deliberate | known false |
| **Enchantment** | **`incantara`** | **awake** | **shared with others in frame** | **external magical action** | **frame-internal consistency; correspondence with waking-normal varies** |

The incantara is **shared** (unlike somnara, which is private). It is **caused** (unlike somnara, which is spontaneous). The experiencer is **awake** (unlike somnara, which requires sleep). The experiencer **may or may not know they are in it** (unlike imaginar, which is by definition known to the projector).

**Truth within the frame can hold.** The roses in the old woman's enchanted garden (Snow Queen III) say `nos in terro fuimus — et il non inter ili erat. Ergo il vivit`. Their report is sound *within the enchanted frame*: the incantara lets flowers travel underground and return with intelligence about the dead. Outside the frame, in strict waking-normal physics, roses do not do this. But the frame-internal report cashed out to waking-normal truth — Kai was in fact alive. The enchantment, that time, told the truth.

This is the load-bearing property of `incantara`: claims drawn from within it are not automatically false (as `imaginar` claims are) and not bounded to a private experiencer (as `somnara` claims are). They are **frame-true**, and their relation to waking-normal truth is a separate question to be resolved by leaving the frame and checking.

## Worked sentences

**Avla incantara horti vetal feminae rosas locar permittat.**

*The enchantment of the old woman's garden permits the roses to speak.*

(Names the incantara as the agent of the altered physics. Without the incantara, roses would not speak; within it, they do. The frame is not described as illusion or trick — it has real causal force on what is possible inside it. `permittat` indicative: this is how the frame operates, not a hypothetical.)

**Incant: rosas in terro fuerunt, et omnal mortuos viderunt, et Kai non inter ili erat. Ergo il vivit.**

*In enchantment: the roses were in the earth, saw all the dead, and Kai was not among them. Therefore he lives.*

(The Snow Queen III rose-line under the new register. The `incant:` particle marks the claim as drawn from the enchanted frame. The roses' knowledge is sound within the frame; the inference (sciar from absence) holds; the conclusion correspondingly holds in waking-normal because the frame, in this case, preserved truth. This is the prototype application: claims drawn from within an incantara may be true, but their evidential status is `incantev` — frame-source — not direct waking observation. A speaker reporting the rose-line outside the frame should mark it `incant:` or use the `incantev` evidential to flag its provenance.)

**Gerda non sciat utrum vidat an in incantara sit.**

*Gerda does not know whether she sees or is in an enchantment.*

(The classical boundary problem, parallel to the somnara version. The enchanted may not know they are enchanted; the language can name the indeterminacy even when the experiencer cannot resolve it. From inside the incantara, the question may be unaskable — exactly as the analogous question is unaskable from inside the somnara. Vendergood gives the outside-frame observer the vocabulary to mark what the inside-frame experiencer cannot.)

## Note

Fifth member of the abstract-feminine `-ara` cluster, completing the altered-frame taxonomy:

- **mnemara** — inner store (faculty of memory)
- **glorara** — outer position (standing in honor)
- **animara** — what is alive (animating principle)
- **somnara** — what is lived-without-authoring during sleep (dream-content)
- **incantara** — magical altered-frame shared with others (enchantment)

Each is Class III, takes `avla`, declines on the vowel-final feminine paradigm. The pattern is now firmly established; further `-ara` coinings should follow without ceremony.

**Future scope.** The Snow Queen has further altered-frame content coming — Story V (Robber Girl: feral-instinct knowing), Story VI (Lapp/Finn: shamanic divination), Story VII (Snow Queen's palace: frozen-rationality frame). The working hypothesis is **unified**: `incantara` is the genus, and specific enchantments — shamanism, frozen-rationality, fairy-conjuring — are species within it, distinguished by adjective (`incantara shamanal`, `incantara glacial`, `incantara horti`) or by context. They share the register marker (`incant:`) and the evidential channel (`incantev`).

The unified hypothesis aligns with Vendergood's compression principle: one structural distinction carrying broad work, not many narrow distinctions duplicating effort. A reader who knows `incantara` can read any altered-frame passage in the language without learning new register vocabulary per story; an adjective or context cue narrows the species without rebuilding the genus.

The unified default holds unless a future translation scene **resists** the genus — that is, unless a specific altered-frame turns out to have load-bearing properties that `incantara` cannot carry. In that case, a dedicated `-ara` noun would be coined for the exception. Until then, the genus stands.
