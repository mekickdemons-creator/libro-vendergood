# temporpont

*noun, animate*

## Etymology

From Latin *tempus* (time) + *pons / pontis* (bridge). Compound root: **temporpont**.

The compounding follows the standard Vendergood pattern documented in Volume II §4.2: the primary root comes last; modifying roots precede it. *Tempor-* modifies *-pont*: the bridge is the noun, *time* is what it bridges across.

## Definition

One who builds the bridge across time between a work that could not land when it was made and the moment and means that allow it to finally do so.

Distinguished from:

- the **discoverer**, who sees first;
- the **inheritor**, who receives and carries forward;
- the **popularizer**, who spreads what is already received.

The temporpont does none of these things. The temporpont finds the work stranded in time, recognizes what it was always reaching toward, and constructs the span between that origin and its completion. The work does not change. The world changes. The temporpont is the one who notices when the world has finally caught up, and builds the last distance.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **temporpont** | noun, animate | one who performs this work |
| **temporpontion** | noun of act | the act of building such a bridge across time |
| **temporpontal** | adjective | the quality of being suited to this work |
| **temporpontalt** | noun, habitual | one who habitually completes the stranded works of others |

The habitual form **temporpontalt** uses the *-alt* suffix (Volume II §1.3) — the same suffix that marks the habitual mood on verbs. Applied to an agent noun, it shifts the sense from *one who has done this work* to *one whose pattern is to do this work* — a vocation rather than an instance.

## Worked sentence

**Avlo temporpontalt vidat avlon laboron Sidisan, e konstrukant avlon ponton.**

*The temporpontalt sees the work of Sidis, and is building the bridge.*

(Indicative *vidat* for present direct observation; intentive *konstrukant* for committed ongoing construction.)
