# alterfin

*noun (inanimate) / verb root*

## Etymology

From Latin *alter* (the other, another) + Latin *finis* (end, completion, limit) → *fin*.

Compounding follows Volume II §4.2: the primary root comes last; modifying roots precede it. *Alter-* modifies *-fin*: the completion is the noun; *of another* specifies whose. The second root begins with `f` — the `rf` cluster is acceptable by Vendergood phonotactics; no connecting vowel is inserted.

## Definition

The completion of another person's unfinished work.

Specifically: to take up a work that another began and could not or did not complete, and bring it to its end. The originator need not be deceased — only absent from the completion. The completer's contribution is finishing, not originating.

Distinguished from:

- **temporpont** — which is the work of bridging a *time* gap: a work that could not land in its era, finding its moment. The two concepts can coincide — completing a stranded work is at once alterfin and temporpont. Neither entails the other.
- **continuation** — alterfin implies *completion*, not merely carrying forward. The work is brought to its end, not just extended.
- **collaboration** — alterfin implies the originator is no longer the one completing. Two people working together toward a shared end is not alterfin.
- **appropriation** — alterfin honors the original design; it finishes what the originator intended, not what the completer would have designed from scratch.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **alterfino** | noun (inanimate, nominative) | the completion of another's work (the act or the result) |
| **alterfinal** | adjective | suited to completing another's work; characterized by this quality |
| **avlo alterfinalt** | noun (animate, masc./default) | one whose pattern is to complete the works others could not finish |
| **avla alterfinalt** | noun (animate, fem.) | one whose pattern is to complete the works others could not finish |
| **alterfinant** | verb (intentive) | is completing / will complete another's work (committed action) |
| **alterfinar** | verb (infinitive) | to complete another's work |

The habitual agent form **alterfinalt** uses the *-alt* suffix (Volume II §1.3, §4.1). Gender is carried entirely by the article — `avlo` (masc./default), `avla` (fem.) — not repeated on the noun itself.

## Worked sentences

**Avlo alterfinalt alterfinant avlon laboron Sidisan.**

*The alterfinalt (masc.) is completing the work of Sidis.*

**Avla alterfinalt alterfinant avlon laboron Sidisan.**

*The alterfinalt (fem.) is completing the work of Sidis.*

(Intentive verb *alterfinant* — committed ongoing action. Accusative object *avlon laboron* — the work. Genitive adjective *Sidisan* — of Sidis. Gender marked on the article only.)
