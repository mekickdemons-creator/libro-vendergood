# finoperalt

*noun, animate*

## Etymology

From Latin *finis* (end, completion) + *opus / operis* (work, labor). Compound root: **finoper**.

The compounding follows the standard Vendergood pattern documented in Volume II §4.2: the primary root comes last; modifying roots precede it. *Fin-* modifies *-oper*: the work is the noun, completion is what is done to it. The habitual agent suffix *-alt* (Volume II §1.3, §4.1) shifts the sense from one who has completed a work to one whose pattern is to complete works — a vocation rather than an instance.

Distinct from **temporpontalt**: the temporpontalt bridges a work across time to the moment of its completion; the finoperalt performs the completion itself. The temporpontalt is the span; the finoperalt is the final labor. In practice the same entity may be both, but the words name different aspects of the same act.

## Definition

One whose vocation is the completion of works left unfinished by others — not inheriting, not popularizing, not discovering, but working through to the end what another began and could not finish.

Distinguished from:

- the **temporpontalt**, who builds the bridge across time between a stranded work and its moment of completion;
- the **inheritor**, who receives and carries forward without necessarily finishing;
- the **continuator**, who extends a work in new directions rather than completing it on its own terms.

The finoperalt holds the original work's terms as inviolate and finishes within them. The measure of the finoperalt's success is that the finished work looks as though its originator could have written the final line.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **finopero** | noun, animate | one who completes a work (instance) |
| **finoperion** | noun of act | the act of completing another's unfinished work |
| **finoperal** | adjective | suited to the completion of unfinished works |
| **finoperalt** | noun, habitual | one whose vocation is completing the works of others |

## Worked sentence

**Avlo finoperalt konstrukant avlon laboron al avlo fino suo.**

*The finoperalt is building the work toward its own end.*

(Intentive *konstrukant* for committed ongoing construction; *al avlo fino suo* — "toward its own end" — the work's inherent completion point, not an externally imposed one.)
