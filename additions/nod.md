# nod

*noun, inanimate*

## Etymology

Latin *nodus* (knot, node; also: difficulty, crux, binding point). Root: **nod**.

The *nodus* in Latin was the knot that tied things together — the point where threads converge and hold. It extended metaphorically to any point of concentration or decision in a system: a knotty problem, a pivotal scene in a drama (*nodus* in rhetorical theory), the central difficulty that must be resolved. In network topology it names what it has always named: the point where paths meet.

Note on brevity: `nod` is an unusually short root for Vendergood's classical base. The brevity is appropriate — the node is the irreducible element of the network, the thing that cannot be further decomposed. The word earns its shortness.

## Definition

A node: a point in a network where paths meet, where transfers are possible, where routes converge and diverge. In transit usage: a stop, station, or hub where travelers may change routes. In abstract usage: any point of structural significance in a connected system — a place where the topology changes.

The nod is what makes the translat possible. Without the nod, there is no point where one iter ends and another begins. The translat is the permission; the nod is the place.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **nodo** | noun, inanimate | a node (instance; this specific stop or junction) |
| **nodion** | noun of act | the act of connecting; a junction event |
| **nodal** | adjective | of or relating to nodes; pivotal; structurally central |
| **nodalt** | noun, habitual | one who manages or studies junctions and hubs |

## Worked sentence

**Avlo nod nodal estat inter avlo iter e avlo iter.**

*The nodal node exists between one route and another.*

(Indicative *estat*: exists, is. *nodal* as attributive adjective modifying *avlo nod*: the node in its node-quality, its junction-nature. *inter avlo iter e avlo iter*: between one route and another — the nod's definition rendered as a sentence. A thing is what it is by where it sits in the rete.)
