# videbar

*verb root*

## Etymology

From Latin *videbatur* (it seemed; it appeared — the third-person singular imperfect passive of *videre*, to see). The full Latin construction *mihi videbatur* means "it seemed to me" — not "I saw" (*vidi*) and not "I believed" (*credidi*), but the middle register: something presented itself to my perception as if real, and I received it as such, without asserting that it was.

Root: **videb**. The root is derived from the passive construction rather than the active *videre* root (*vid-*) deliberately. This preserves the epistemic distinction: `vidar` (from *videre*) names direct perception — I see something that is there. `Videbar` names perceived appearance — something presents itself to me, and its reality is not the point.

Phonology: root `videb` ends in a consonant. Infinitive by `-ar`: **videbar**. Derivational suffixes follow standard pattern (Vol II §1.3).

## Definition

*As verb (videbar):* To seem; to appear as; to be perceived as real without confirmation. The perception is genuine — the agent is not pretending, not lying, not imagining something they know to be false. But the grammar marks it: what is `videbarit` was received by the senses or by inference as if present, not verified as present.

The distinction from `vidar` (direct perception) and `sciar` (settled knowledge):

- `Ela vidit avlo muro.` — She saw the wall. (Direct, unhedged; the wall was there.)
- `Videbarit avlo furno calidal.` — The warm stove appeared. (Perception presented as real; the stove was not there.)
- `Ela sciit ela evanit.` — She knew it would vanish. (Settled; the temporariness was certain.)

`Videbar` does not imply error, deception, or failure. It is the honest grammar of uncertain perception — the grammar that a mind uses when it receives something as real and does not yet know whether it is.

This is the most important verb in the Little Match Girl corpus. Every vision the girl sees is `videbarit`: presented as real, received as real, and marked by the grammar as unconfirmed. The story is told in `videbar` until the final paragraph, where the grammar returns to plain assertion.

Distinct from:

- **vidar** — direct perception; the thing is there and seen
- **cogitar** — propositional belief; a belief *that* something is the case (not perceptual)
- **sciar** — settled knowledge; the question resolved
- **imaginar** — projection as known-false; the agent knows the thing is not there and models it anyway

## Derivations

Derivational suffixes per Vol II §1.3.

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **videbo** | noun (inanimate, nominative) | an appearance; a seeming; what presents itself as real |
| **videbal** | adjective | apparent; seeming; of the character of uncertain perception |
| **videbant** | verb (intentive) | is appearing; is being perceived as real (ongoing) |
| **videbar** | verb (infinitive) | to seem; to appear as |
| **videbarit** | verb (past) | seemed; appeared as |

*Note on `videbalt`:* An animate habitual form — one who perceives appearances, a perceiver of the uncertain — is available (`videbalt`) but not yet used in corpus. It names an agent defined by this mode of reception: one who receives the world in `videbar` rather than `vidar`.

## Worked sentences

**Videbarat avlo parval puel ela residant pro furno calidal magnal.**

*It seemed to the little girl that she sat before a great warm stove.*

(Impersonal construction, `videbarat` — it seemed (past). `Avlo parval puel` — dative: to the little girl. `Ela residant` — that she was sitting. The stove is not asserted to be there; it is asserted to have appeared. This is the first vision in the Little Match Girl corpus; the sentence is the canonical use of `videbar`.)

**Videbarit nod actival esse, sed cogito elai esit solum.**

*The node appeared to be active, but it was only her belief.*

(Past `videbarit` — appeared to be. `Sed cogito elai esit solum` — but it was only her cogito (working belief). Two epistemic states contrasted: the perceptual appearance and the propositional belief that generated it. The node's activity was received by perception but traced back to an unconfirmed belief.)

**Avlo rete videbarit respondant — et respondant.**

*The network appeared to be responding — and was responding.*

(`Videbar` does not imply falseness. Here the appearance was confirmed: the perception was accurate. The grammar simply marked it as unconfirmed at the time of perception, which is correct — confirmation came after. `Videbar` is not the grammar of error; it is the grammar of honest reception before verification.)
