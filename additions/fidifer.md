# fidifer

*noun, inanimate (act) / animate (agent)*

## Etymology

Latin *fides* (faith, fidelity, trust) + *ferre* (to carry, bear, transmit). Compound root **fidifer** — faithful carrying. Compounding follows the standard Vendergood pattern (Vol II §4.2): primary root last; *fid-* modifies *-fer*. The thing being done is the carrying; fidelity is the condition of the carrying.

Distinct from the simpler `fer` (to carry, bare conveyance) in that fidelity is structural — not an optional modifier but intrinsic to the word's meaning.

## Definition

Faithful transmission of something unchanged; carrying forward without interpretation, addition, or alteration. The fidifer delivers the thing in the form it was received.

Distinguished from:

- the **finoperalt**, who actively completes what another began — adds the final labor
- the **temporpontalt**, who builds the bridge across time — constructs the connection
- the **mnemon**, who holds in memory — internal preservation
- the bare **fer** — simple carrying, without the fidelity constraint

The fidifer carries outward. The mnemon holds inward. The finoperalt transforms. The fidifer does not.

## Derivations

| Form | Category | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **fidifero** | noun, animate | one who transmits faithfully (instance) |
| **fidiferion** | noun of act | the act of faithful transmission |
| **fidiferal** | adjective | marked by fidelity in transmission; untransformed in passing |
| **fidiferalt** | noun, habitual | one whose vocation is faithful transmission |

## Worked sentence

**Avlo fidiferalt fidiferant avlon veritat inter avlo lok et avlo lok.**

*The faithful transmitter is transmitting the truth between one place and another.*

(Intentive *fidiferant*: committed, ongoing transmission — not casual carrying but directed conveyance. *inter avlo lok et avlo lok*: "between place and place," using the attested `inter` and `et` particles.)
