## Historia Unal — Speculo et Splintoi Eius
## First Story — The Mirror and Its Fragments

*Danish: Første Historie, som handler om Spejlet og Spejlstykkerne*

---

**Nun incipat nos. Quan nos pervenat ad finem historiae, nos sciarut plus quan nun sciat; nam avlo troldo malal esit — un de omnal malissimal — esit "Diablo!"**

*Now we begin. When we reach the end of the story, we shall know more than we know now; for it was a wicked troll — one of the very worst — it was "the Devil!"*

---

**Un die, il in humore bono veral erat, nam il fabricit ivlo speculo quod habitit hanc proprietatem: omnal bonum et belal quod in eo speculit, fere evanit ad nihilum; sed id quod non valebat et turpal apparit, id prominit et turpior fiit.**

*One day he was in very good humor, for he had made a mirror with this property: everything good and beautiful that was reflected in it nearly vanished to nothing; but that which was worthless and looked ugly stood out and became even worse.*

---

**Regionoi belissimal in eo simil spinacea coctal erant, et homines bonissimal turpissimal fiit aut in capite stabant sine ventro; faciei tam distortal erant ut non cognoscibiles; et si quis maculam habitit, certal erat eam supra nasom et orem cucurrit. Vel amusabilal esit, dicit Diablo.**

*The most beautiful landscapes looked like boiled spinach in it; the best people became hideous or stood on their heads without stomachs; faces were so distorted they were unrecognizable; and if anyone had a freckle, they could be sure it had spread over nose and mouth. It was terribly amusing, said the Devil.*

---

**Quan cogitio pial et bonal per hominem transalt, ridor in speculo apparat, ita ut troldo supra inventionelo artifical ridat. Omnes qui ad scholam trolde ibant — nam troldo scholam trolde habitit — narrant quod miraculo factum esit: nunc demum, putant, potit vidar quomodo mundus et homines vere apparant.**

*When a pious and good thought passed through a person, a grin appeared in the mirror, so that the troll laughed over his artful invention. All who went to the troll's school — for the troll had a troll school — said that a wonder had occurred: now at last, they thought, one could see how the world and people truly look.*

---

**Currant cum speculo, et tandem terra nec homo erat quod distortum per illud non esit. Tunc etiam ad caelum volant cum eo, ad deridar angelos et Deum. Quo altior cum speculo volant, eo magis ridat; vix illud tenebant; altior et altior volant, propius Deum et angelos — tunc speculo tam terribiliter de ridore tremat ut ex manibus eorum labit et ad terram cadit, ubi ino centum millionia, billionia, et adhuc plural fragmentoi frangit; et inde longe magnior calamitio fecit quan ante.**

*They ran about with the mirror, and at last there was no land and no person that had not been distorted through it. Then they intended to fly up to heaven with it, to mock the angels and God. The higher they flew with the mirror, the more it grinned; they could hardly hold it; higher and higher they flew, nearer to God and the angels — then the mirror trembled so terribly from the grinning that it slipped from their hands and fell to earth, where it shattered into a hundred million, a billion, and still more fragments; and from this it caused far greater calamity than before.*

---

**Nam alquoi fragmentoi vix tan magnal erant quan grano arenal; et haec ino mundum latum volit et ino ocloi hominum venit, et ibi sedit et homines omnal obliqual vidant, aut in ocloi eorum pullulant; ivlum fragmento minutissimal eandem potentio retinuit quan totum speculo habitit. Alqui homines etiam fragmento minutal ino cordo acceperunt — et id vel triste erat: ivlo cordo fiit simil glaciofragmento.**

*For some fragments were hardly as large as a grain of sand; and these flew about in the wide world and came into people's eyes, and sat there and made people see everything crookedly, or they swarmed in people's eyes; even the tiniest fragment retained the same power that the whole mirror had. Some people even received a tiny fragment in their heart — and that was truly sad: the heart became like a piece of ice.*

---

**Alquoi fragmentoi speculo tam magnal erant ut pro fenestros usurparit — sed malum erat amicos per haec fenestros videre; alial fragmentoi ino ocularioi venit — et malum erat quan homines haec ocularioi posuit ad recte vidar et justal esse. Diablo ridat ita ut in ventre eloe titillat — tam suaval erat ei. Sed adhuc, parval fragmentoi vitri in aero volit. Nos audiut de eis!**

*Some fragments of the mirror were so large that they were used as windowpanes — but it was bad to see one's friends through these panes; other fragments came into spectacles — and it was bad when people put on these spectacles to see properly and be fair. The Devil laughed so that it tickled in his stomach — it was so pleasantly delightful for him. But still, small fragments of glass flew in the air. We shall hear of them!*

---

### Note on Epistemic Register — Historia Unal

This story establishes the mirror as an instrument of corrupted perception — the central mechanism of everything that follows.

**vidat vs. videbar:** The eye-fragment passage uses plain **vidat** (neutral seeing), not **videbar** (seeming/uncertain perception). The person with a fragment in the eye is not uncertain about what they see — they simply see wrong. **videbar** would incorrectly imply they recognize the distortion as uncertainty; the horror of the shard is that they do not.

**Habitual mood at the mirror:** `transalt` — the thought *habitually* passes through a person, and *habitually* a grin appears. The `-alt` suffix marks this as a recurring pattern, not a single event. The troll's mirror is not triggered once; it responds to every instance of goodness, always.

**Ontological transformation at the heart:** `ivlo cordo fiit simil glaciofragmento` — the heart *became* like a piece of ice, not *seemed* like ice. The transformation is real. A shard in the heart does not make a person *believe* they feel nothing; it makes them *actually feel* nothing. There is no epistemic access to correct it from the inside.

**The mirror's inversion of the sight hierarchy:** What would normally ground **vidar** (clear, confirmed perception) now produces distorted **vidat** (mere looking). The mirror converts seeing into seeming without the perceiver's knowledge. This is the setup for Kai's condition in Historia Duo.

## Historia Duo — Parval Puer et Parval Puella
## Second Story — A Little Boy and a Little Girl

*Danish: Anden Historie. En lille Dreng og en lille Pige*

---

### I. The Two Children

**In avla magnal urbo, ubi tot domoi et homines erant ut non quis horto habere potit, plurimal homines floribus in vasoi contentit. Sed duo parval pauperal infantoi ibi vivant, qui horto paulo magnior quan vaso floral habuerunt.**

*In the big city, where there were so many houses and people that not everyone could have a garden, most people contented themselves with flowers in pots. But two poor little children lived there, who had a garden somewhat larger than a flower pot.*

**Avlo puer Kai et avla puella Gerda non frater et soror erant — sed se invicem amat tan multal quan si sic estot. Parentoi eorum in mansardoi adjacental vivant, in domoi du ubi tegumento uno tangat alterum et canalo aquatical inter currit. De quaque domo parval fenestro aperabat ino canalum — et quis solum supra canalo stepat de fenestro ad fenestro.**

*Kai the boy and Gerda the girl were not brother and sister — but they loved each other as much as if they were. Their parents lived in adjacent attics, in two houses where one roof touched the other and a water gutter ran between them. From each house a small window opened onto the gutter — and one stepped only over the gutter, from window to window.*

**Parentoi avlam magnal arcaron lignal inter fenestras posuit, et in quaque arcaron herboi pro uso et parval frutico rosal crescit — rosa una in arcaron Kai-e, rosa una in arcaron Gerda-e. Avlas rosas taliter crescant ut arcoi rosal supra fenestras fierent. Kai et Gerda inter rosas ludant, et felices erant simil frater et soror.**

*The parents placed a large wooden box between the windows, and in each box herbs for use and a little rose-bush grew — one rose in Kai's box, one in Gerda's. The roses grew in such a way that they formed arches of roses above the windows. Kai and Gerda played among the roses, and were happy like brother and sister.*

---

### II. The Grandmother's Stories

**Hiemo veniat, et avlas rosas mortuat — sed Kai et Gerda non tristal erant. Avla avia Kai-e, vetal et amabilal, fabulas narrat dum nivo cadant et vento sibilat foras.**

*Winter came, and the roses died — but Kai and Gerda were not sad. Kai's grandmother, old and kind, told stories while snow fell and wind hissed outside.*

**"Avlas albas apes volant," avia dicit.**

*"The white bees are flying," said the grandmother.*

**"Avlas habent reginam etiam?" Kai rogat.**

*"Do they too have a queen?" Kai asked.*

**"Sic," avia respondat, "avla Nivregina. Ela inter floccoi magnissimal volat, et per vias urbi, et per fenestras spectat — et fenestras cum gelu et floribus glacial pingit."**

*"Yes," she answered, "the Snow Queen. She flies among the largest flakes, and through the streets of the city, and looks through the windows — and paints the windows with frost and ice-flowers."*

**Avla nocte, dum Kai ante fenestro stat, gelu crassal fenestro coperat. Kai foramen parval ino gelu calefit, et per id foras vidat. Floccoi nival cadant; et un floco crescere et crescere videbarat, donec ino feminam fiit — claral et belal, sed glacial omnino. Ocloi simil stelloi du erant, sed non quietal. Avla nutit ad Kai — et Kai timit. De fenestro saltit. Avlo magnal floco foras volat.**

*That night, as Kai stood in front of the window, thick frost covered the pane. Kai warmed a small hole into the frost, and saw through it outside. Snowflakes were falling; and one flake seemed to grow and grow, until it became a woman — bright and beautiful, but wholly of ice. Her eyes were like two stars, but not still. She nodded to Kai — and Kai was afraid. He leapt from the window. The great flake flew away outside.*

---

### III. The Shards

**Multal die post, in aestate calidal, dum Kai et Gerda inter rosas ludant et imagoi coloral in librum magnal vidant, Kai subito clamat: "Ah! Aliquid ino oclum igne intrit!" Gerda eum capit et ino oclum eius spectat — sed nihil videt.**

*Many days later, in warm summer, while Kai and Gerda played among the roses and looked at colored pictures in a great book, Kai suddenly cried: "Ah! Something has gone into my eye!" Gerda took him and looked into his eye — but saw nothing.*

**"Ignat cogitat quod evanit," Kai dicit. Sed non evanit. Avlo splinto speculi malal — quod in Historia Unal de manibus diabloe cadit — ino oclum eius intrit. Et simul, alia splinto minutissimal ino cordo eius intrit. Et cordo eius simil glaciofragmento fiit.**

*"I think it has gone," Kai said. But it had not. A shard of the evil mirror — which in the First Story fell from the devil's hands — had entered his eye. And at the same moment, another tiny shard entered his heart. And his heart became like a piece of ice.*

---

### IV. The Transformation

**Statim Kai mutat. Ad Gerda spectat et dicit: "Tu turpissimal videt cum lacrimoi." Et librum sua spectat: "Avlas imagoi puerilal sunt. Nihilum belal in eis." Et frutico rosal — quod ante amat — Kai laceravit et ino terro calcavit, ridens.**

*At once Kai changed. He looked at Gerda and said: "You look ugliest with tears." And he looked at his book: "These pictures are childish. There is nothing beautiful in them." And the rose-bush — which he had loved before — Kai tore up and trampled into the earth, laughing.*

**Avla avia fabulas narrare temptat — sed Kai voces eius mimat et ridat. Ela imitat dum ambulat. Omnal quod ante eum delectabat, nunc Kai irridat. Splinto in oclo: omnal bonum et belal turpal vidat. Splinto in cordo: pro hominas quas ante amavit, nihilum sentit.**

*The grandmother tried to tell stories — but Kai mocked her voice and laughed. He imitated her as he walked. Everything that had delighted him before, now Kai mocked. The shard in his eye: everything good and beautiful he saw as ugly. The shard in his heart: for the people he had loved before, he felt nothing.*

---

### V. The Abduction

**Hiemo iterum venit. Kai cum alial pueroi ino plateam exit, cum parval trahea lignal. Pueroi trahas suas ad raedoi magnal adjungant, et raedoi pueros per plateas ducant. Subito magnal trahea albal venit, et auriga vestimento nival vestit. Kai traham suam ad avla albal trahea adjungit. Et trahea de urbo eum abducit — ino campos et montoi nival.**

*Winter came again. Kai went out with the other boys into the square, with a little wooden sled. The boys would attach their sleds to grand carriages, and the carriages would pull the boys through the squares. Suddenly a great white sleigh came, and the driver clad in snowy garments. Kai attached his sled to the white sleigh. And the sleigh carried him away from the city — into snowy fields and mountains.*

**Kai timit et Pater Noster recitare temptat — sed solum tabulam multiplicationis recordat. Floccoi nival magnal fiant — tan magnal quan pulli albal — et tandem auriga stat, vestimento cadit, et Kai vidat avla Nivregina ipsa.**

*Kai was afraid and tried to recite the Lord's Prayer — but he could only recall the multiplication table. The snowflakes grew large — large as white chickens — and at last the driver stopped, the garments fell, and Kai saw the Snow Queen herself.*

**"Frigal es," ela dicit, et lo basat ino fronte. Avlo basio frigal simil glacio ino cordo eius intrit — et cordo, jam glacial, glacialior fiit. Sed Kai non timit jam. Ela belal videbarat ei, et omnia quod Kai sciar potit ante — Gerda, avia, rosas — evanit ex memoria eius.**

*"You are cold," she said, and kissed him on the forehead. The cold kiss like ice went into his heart — and the heart, already icy, became icier still. But Kai was no longer afraid. She seemed beautiful to him, and all that Kai had been able to know before — Gerda, the grandmother, the roses — vanished from his memory.*

---

### Note on Epistemic Register — Historia Duo

Historia Duo is the dramatic center of the corrupted-perception mechanism set up in Historia Unal. The shards enter Kai — one in his eye, one in his heart — and his epistemic apparatus is inverted in real time. The story exercises the full **vidar / videbar / vidat / sciar** hierarchy because Kai moves through all four states.

**Snow Queen at the window — videbar:** When Kai first sees the Snow Queen through the frost-hole, the text uses `videbarat` ("seemed"): one snowflake *seemed* to grow into a woman. This is the correct register: at this stage Kai is still epistemically intact, and the perception is genuinely uncertain. He cannot vidar her clearly through the frost; what he experiences is videbar, and the grammar marks it.

**The shards — vidar inverted:** After the shards enter, Kai no longer has access to vidar. The text uses plain `vidat` for his post-shard perception: `omnal bonum et belal turpal vidat` — "everything good and beautiful he saw as ugly." He sees, but the seeing produces wrong content. The vidar/videbar/vidat hierarchy is functionally collapsed for him: he can no longer distinguish clear perception from distorted perception.

**Memory and the past — sciar:** Kai's loss at the end is marked with `omnia quod Kai sciar potit ante — Gerda, avia, rosas — evanit ex memoria eius`. What he had been able to *know* (sciar — certain knowledge), the people and roses he loved — vanishes. The shard does not just corrupt present perception; it severs the memory of having had it. This is more total than the eye-shard alone: even the trace of having known is gone.

**The Snow Queen at the end — videbar restored, but inverted in valence:** When Kai sees the Snow Queen at the end, the text says `belal videbarat ei` — she *seemed* beautiful to him. Videbar is back, but doing different work. He is no longer uncertain about *what* he sees but about *what it means*. The cold kiss is real and registers correctly — he reads it as beautiful. Videbar (uncertain perception) is now serving distortion rather than honest epistemic hedging. This is the harder failure mode: not blindness, but seeming-real that is wrong.
